About Our Indigenous Language Development Project

The project aims to reduce the knowledge and language gap by making Wikipedia-style information available in Indian indigenous and regional languages.

Why This Project?

Wikipedia has more than 5 million articles in English, but most people in India do not take full advantage of it. Almost 90% of Indians study in their mother tongue or Hindi. Because of this, they miss out on the vast amount of knowledge available online.

The Knowledge Gap



  • India receives 117 million Wikipedia views per day, but these views are mostly from English users (only 10% of the population).
  • 90% of people cannot access or understand the English Wikipedia properly.
  • This creates a large knowledge gap between English users and native speakers.
  • Countries like Japan show how powerful a native language Wikipedia can be (90% of Japanese use Wikipedia).

Project Purpose

The project aims to make knowledge easily accessible to people who speak indigenous, tribal and regional languages â€‹â€‹in India. Most important information on the Internet today, especially on Wikipedia, is available in English. But the reality is that a large area of India cannot read English comfortably. Because of this, they miss out on important knowledge that can help them learn, grow and improve their lives.

We want to solve this problem by creating a platform where people can read high quality information in their native language. When knowledge comes in language that people understand, it becomes more meaningful, more useful and more accessible.

The project is inspired by the IndicWiki initiative of IIIT Hyderabad, which aims to create Wikipedia content in Indian native languages. We want to take this idea further by creating a simple, friendly and easy-to-use website for indigenous and tribal communities. Our goal is not only to translate knowledge, but also to make it culturally relevant, understandable and useful for students of all backgrounds.

We also want to make sure the process of creating content is easy. Community members, students, teachers and volunteers can all participate in contributing articles, stories, local history, cultural knowledge and teaching materials in their own language. This will help to build a strong and active community.

The aim of this project is also to reduce the digital divide. There is a big difference between those who have access to English skills and those who do not. By giving them access to information in the language they know, we aim to bring equality to learning, education and solving real world problems.

Project Objectives

  • Document all learning and methods used in the IndiWiki project.
  • Create a platform that facilitates information for indigenous communities.
  • Support all Indian languages, not only Telugu or Hindi.
  • Reduce the digital divide and the knowledge divide between tribal communities.
  • Encourage more local language contributions from community members.

Features of Our Website

  • Easy to read articles in local languages
    The website provides information written in simple and clear language that local people can easily understand. The articles are designed with the reading styles of indigenous, tribal and regional communities in mind. The content is not complex, and avoid heavy technical words . This helps people of all educational levels to learn without difficulty.
  • culture and society-based knowledge
    Along with general knowledge, the website contains special sections that highlight the unique culture, traditions, lifestyle and heritage of various communities. It gives space to local stories, folk knowledge, practices, festivals and history that are not documented on mainstream platforms. This helps preserve cultural identity and allows local communities to see themselves represented online.
  • Simple user interface for tribal and agricultural users
    The website design is kept very clean, simple and easy to navigate. Buttons, menus and options are large and easy to identify. Even first-time internet users from tribal and rural areas can use it without any confusion. The user interface focuses on minimal text, clear icons and a friendly structure without distractions.
  • Support for multiple scripts and languages
    There are many languages and scripts in India. This website supports multiple scripts so that users can read the content in their native language. Be it Telugu, Hindi, Marathi, Bengali, Santali (Ol Chiki), Gondi or any other indigenous or regional script, the platform is built in such a way that different languages â€‹â€‹can be easily added in the future.

Community Impact

This project will help millions of indigenous and Indian language speakers access educational resources. Students, teachers, tribal communities and local researchers will be able to read and understand the knowledge in their mother tongue.

Future Scope

  • AI-based translation and article suggestion tools.
  • Community driven content development.
  • Offline mode for rural areas with less Internet.
  • Workshops and awareness programs for indigenous communities.
